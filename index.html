Ch. 6 — Either/Or, Unless/Until/Except, Complex Conditional Chains
You're here now. This chapter extends conditional logic into the harder indicator words the LSAT loves to hide (unless, until, except, only if, neither/nor). Master these translations cold — they appear in SA, NA, Flaw, and Weaken questions constantly. Complete all exercises in the chapter before moving on.
🔗 7Sage alignment: Wk 2 (Quantifiers/Sets) → Wk 3 (MBT, SA) — Ch. 6 primes your conditional reasoning for these question types
📖 Phase 1: Foundations — You Are Here (Week 2)
Follow 7Sage's daily lesson plan exactly. Your job is deep understanding, not speed. Loophole chapters run parallel — finish Ch. 6 this week, Ch. 7 next week, working through to Ch. 12 by ~Apr 5. The two resources reinforce each other chapter by chapter.
🎯 Phase 2: Practice Blocks — Apr 6 onwards
7Sage now drives your daily content via assigned drill blocks and weekly PTs every Sunday. Loophole Ch. 12 should be complete by the time you enter Phase 2 — use it as a reference during error log review, not new reading.

Openings + Deep Game Analysis
1 hr · afternoon
📚
Opening Study0–20 min
Commit to ONE opening each side for months: London System (white) and Caro-Kann (black). Both are strategically rich, beginner-friendly, and built on long-term positional ideas — perfect for your specific gap. Study the ideas behind each move, not memorized lines.
🎮
1 Slow Game + Deep Analysis20–60 min
One 15+10 or 30-min game. Spend as long analyzing it as you did playing. Ask: where did the position shift? What was the critical moment? Find your own mistakes before the engine. One well-analyzed game beats five unreviewed ones every time.
